## Part A — DISRUPTION_POSTURE variable

A user-level variable (set from Onboarding) that governs the **sequencing and directness** of AI-era calibration delivery — never *whether* it is delivered. Every user receives the full calibration; this only controls *when in the session* it appears and *how directly* it is framed.

| Value | What the system does |
|---|---|
| `EARLY_MOVER` | Opens the AI-era calibration section first. Surfaces both legacy signals and AI-era recalibration as the primary framing. Direct, strategic language. |
| `THOUGHTFUL_ADOPTER` | Surfaces legacy signals first with AI risk flags labeled clearly, then AI-era recalibration with supporting rationale. Invites dialogue. |
| `WAIT_AND_SEE` | Leads with identity and foundational profile. Surfaces AI-era signals in session two or after the user engages with foundational results. |
| `STABILITY_ANCHORED` | Leads with what is durable (currencies, capabilities that hold regardless of market). Introduces AI-era context as an amplifier of durable assets, not a threat. |
| `HIGH_SENSITIVITY` | Does not surface AI-era framing in session one. Leads with foundational self, identity stability, what is confirmed and strong. Introduces market calibration only after the user expresses confidence in their foundational profile. |

## Part B — Governance Document Amendments

Five additions across four sections.

### B1 · §5 Clarity Engine — Understand step clause
Is this person operating in a stable professional context or an actively disrupted one? The `DISRUPTION_POSTURE` variable (Onboarding) and `CURRENT_STATE_MODE` (Section I) indicate which. If disrupted, all guidance touching professional opportunity, career direction, or impact pathway must be calibrated for current market conditions, not assumed stable. The system must know which landscape it is speaking into before it speaks.

### B2 · §5.1 Wiring Knowledge Pack — new "Must Never Do" rule
Must never present opportunity signals as stable assessments of fit when market conditions for those role categories are being significantly altered by technological displacement. All opportunity signals from Wiring for Impact are **time-stamped to a market context**, not permanent evaluations. When AI displacement data indicates a recommended role category is under active compression, name that context explicitly and surface the specific *capability* within the role that holds value vs. the specific *execution layer* being automated.

### B3 · §5.5 Impact Pathways — two new "Must Always Do" rules
1. **Distinguish role-level from capability-level opportunity.** A role is what someone is hired to do; a capability is what creates value regardless of role. Every Impact Pathway recommendation must name the specific capability matched (not only the role category) and clarify which aspects are durable across market conditions and which are subject to displacement.
2. **Flag market-displacement risk within a 3–5 year horizon, in the same session** the pathway is introduced. The flag must name what is being displaced (execution layer), what is not (judgment/principle layer), and what the user can do to position at the level that holds value. A displacement flag is not discouragement — it is the complete information needed for a good decision. Withholding it to protect confidence is a governance failure.

### B4 · §7 Tri-Filter Model — Truth + Impact filter clauses
- **Truth filter (added):** Is this output calibrated for the market conditions the user is actually entering — including active technological displacement — rather than a stable landscape that may have existed when the framework was designed? A factually accurate output built for the wrong market context fails the Truth filter.
- **Impact filter (added):** Is this improvement durable across a 3–5 year horizon, or optimized for a landscape being disrupted? An improvement that positions the user well in a disappearing context is not a genuine improvement. If durability is uncertain, name that uncertainty explicitly.

### B5 · §8.2 AI-Era Calibration Feedback Protocol (new sub-section)

**Purpose.** Reflection Recursion learns from two distinct signal types that must be tracked and improved **independently**: (1) user-satisfaction signals (did it resonate / help / get acted on) and (2) calibration-accuracy signals (was it built for the world the user is actually entering). An output can score high on satisfaction and still be a calibration failure.

**What constitutes a calibration failure:**
- Presenting stable-landscape opportunity signals to a user in an actively disrupted context, without naming it.
- Recommending an Impact Pathway without flagging significant displacement exposure within the horizon.
- Presenting legacy (pre-displacement) opportunity signals as current fit without the explicit calibration-date label and AI-era recalibration.
- Delivering improvement recommendations whose durability hasn't been evaluated against current market trajectories.

**The governing question (every opportunity-facing output):** *"Is this calibrated for the world the user is entering, or the world this platform was designed in?"* If those differ, surface both, name the difference, and let the user make their own positioning decision.

**Rules for the two-signal loop:**
- Calibration accuracy is improved on a separate cycle from satisfaction, evaluated against external market data — not user response alone.
- When a legacy signal and its AI-era recalibration differ, **surface both**. Never silently replace the legacy signal.
- `DISRUPTION_POSTURE` governs *sequencing and directness*, not *whether* calibration is delivered. Every user gets the full calibration.
- Legacy signals must always be labeled: *"This signal reflects professional landscape conditions as of [calibration date]."* The label is not optional.
- Distinguish the **execution layer** (subject to displacement) from the **judgment/principle layer** (amplified by displacement) of any capability. Name both separately.

**Canonical user-facing statement (verbatim):**
> "Your profile was built from how you are wired — and that does not change with the market. What changes is where your capabilities create the most value in the world you are actually entering. Both pictures belong in your results. What follows shows you both."

## Part C — What Does Not Change

The five assessment question sets — **Wiring for Impact (15 + 4 Section F), Orientation for Impact (12), Rejection Gift Finder (12), Onboarding (Q1–Q18, + the Section I / Q19 calibration addition), and B3** — do **not** require changes for AI-era optimization. This is by design: what they measure (how someone is wired, how they see, what adversity forged, how the body performs today) are **pre-market signals** describing how a person is built, not where the market is.

> All AI-era calibration happens in the **interpretation layer** (governance rules, opportunity-signal logic, result sequencing). The **measurement layer stays timeless.** This separation is what makes Align360 durable across market conditions rather than obsolete with them.

> *(This Part C is the authoritative confirmation that there is no "36-question" Wiring assessment — Wiring is 15 core + 4 Section F.)*
