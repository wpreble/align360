V · Collision Zones

Where you will predictably pull in different directions

These are not failures — they are predictable tensions that every high-performing partnership navigates. The value is naming them before they arrive, not discovering them under pressure.

High · Pre-establish protocol
Truth Delivery — you both want honesty, you deliver it differently

Both profiles have Truth-Seeker in their orientation. Samuel's Realist gift delivers truth structurally and directly — he will name what is wrong clearly and want to move. Drew's Guardian orientation delivers truth relationally — he will want to ensure the person is safe enough to receive it before it lands. Under time pressure, this gap widens. Samuel will feel Drew is softening something that needs to be said. Drew will feel Samuel is delivering something that isn't landing.

Protocol: Drew leads the delivery conversation. Samuel provides the content. Establish this sequencing before it is needed — not in the middle of a difficult conversation. The rule: Samuel tells Drew what needs to be said. Drew determines when and how it lands.
High · Monitor actively
Stress Disclosure — one absorbs silently, one names it

Drew's stress pattern is silent absorption — he continues giving while running on empty, and all three of his signals reinforce this. The Supporter absorbs what is not his. The Guardian carries others' pain. The Emotional Connector feels it at close range. Samuel's stress pattern is internal processing before external engagement — he goes quiet to think, then names. Under stress, Samuel will eventually name what he is carrying. Drew may not — until the depletion is significant. Samuel may not notice Drew's state until it is already advanced.

Protocol: Build a regular check-in that does not depend on voluntary disclosure. Weekly, simple, direct: "What are you carrying right now that I don't know about?" Neither person waits for the other to raise it. The check-in is the mechanism — not the relationship.
Medium · Name it early
Pace Under Pressure — deliberate meets action-oriented

Samuel's Organizer/Realist combination under pressure first diagnoses then reframes then acts — all three steps before moving. Drew's Supporter/Wise Observer combination first stabilizes people then reads the room then responds. Together the combined sequence is exceptional — but it is slow relative to fast-moving environments. In a startup context, both profiles may be slower than the market requires at specific moments. Neither is naturally reactive.

Protocol: Identify in advance which decisions require the full sequence and which require a fast call. For fast calls: Samuel decides with Drew's one-question check ("who does this affect and how?"). For full-sequence decisions: the natural combined process runs. Don't apply the full sequence to fast-call moments.

What this partnership consistently underweights

Gap · Resource mobilization

The partnership diagnoses, builds, connects, and holds people through it. But neither profile's primary drive is to scale fast, mobilize significant capital, or grow beyond what principled execution allows. The Enterpriser gap is real.

Gap · Execution velocity
Depth and care will occasionally cost you speed

Both profiles are deliberate. Neither is reactive. In a market that sometimes rewards speed over correctness, this is both a strength and a vulnerability. The partnership will produce better outcomes — but occasionally slower than the window allows.

Action: pre-establish which decisions are fast-call and which are full-sequence — before pressure arrives
Risk · Drew's depletion
The person most likely to deplete without signaling

Drew's triple-absorption pattern (Supporter + Guardian + Emotional Connector) means he is statistically the higher depletion risk in this partnership. Samuel's profile will not naturally notice until Drew's output drops. By then the cost is already significant.

Protocol: Samuel initiates weekly check-in. Drew does not wait to be asked twice.
Risk · Truth-delivery timing
The most likely source of unspoken tension

Protocol: named, agreed sequencing — Samuel provides content, Drew leads delivery — decided before the conversation, not during it.
