# Orientation for Impact

*12 questions · ~4 minutes · Single choice · Covers: situation reading, complexity thinking, prioritization, communication, conflict, curiosity*


## Section A — How You Read Situations


### Q1 — New team observation

> You walk into a new team for the first time. Within the first 10 minutes, what do you find yourself paying most attention to?

- **A.** The unspoken dynamics — who defers to whom, where the tension lives → Truth-Seeker
- **B.** Whether the work being done actually makes sense strategically → Builder
- **C.** What's not working and what needs to be fixed or built → Builder/Starter
- **D.** Who seems capable, who's struggling, and what the energy is like → Supporter
- **E.** Whether the group has a clear direction or is drifting → Truth-Seeker/Explainer

### Q2 — Idea pitch reaction

> Someone pitches you an exciting new idea. Before they finish, what question is already forming in your mind?

- **A.** Is this actually true — where are the assumptions or blind spots? → Truth-Seeker
- **B.** Who does this affect and what will it mean for the people involved? → Supporter
- **C.** What would it take to actually build and execute this? → Starter/Builder
- **D.** Where could this lead if it works — what's the long-term potential? → Builder
- **E.** What's the gap this fills that nothing else currently fills? → Truth-Seeker/Explainer

### Q3 — Dense report review

> You're given a dense 40-page report to review. What are you actually looking for as you read?

- **A.** The logical structure — does the argument actually hold together? → Truth-Seeker/Explainer
- **B.** The human implications — who is affected and how? → Supporter
- **C.** The three things I can act on when I'm done reading → Starter
- **D.** The strategic direction it implies — what is this really saying about where we're going? → Builder
- **E.** The assumptions underneath the conclusions — what are they not questioning? → Truth-Seeker

## Section B — How You Think Through Complexity


### Q4 — Complex situation response

> A situation you're responsible for has become genuinely complicated. What does your mind do first?

- **A.** Break it into components and analyze each one separately → Truth-Seeker/Explainer
- **B.** Talk it through with someone I trust — I think better out loud → Supporter
- **C.** Identify the most important lever and focus there — simplify ruthlessly → Starter
- **D.** Step back and look for the pattern underneath all the complexity → Builder/Truth-Seeker
- **E.** Imagine multiple futures and work backwards from the best one → Builder

### Q5 — Disagreement mediation

> Two smart people on your team have opposite views on how to move forward. What do you do?

- **A.** Ask each of them to state their assumptions — the disagreement is usually there → Truth-Seeker
- **B.** Mediate: make sure both feel heard before any decision is made → Supporter
- **C.** Make a call — prolonged debate costs more than a slightly wrong decision → Starter
- **D.** Reframe the question — maybe they're both right and solving the wrong problem → Builder/Explainer
- **E.** Evaluate which view aligns better with where we're trying to go → Builder

## Section C — What You Prioritize


### Q6 — Competing priorities

> You have three competing priorities and can only move one forward today. How do you decide which one?

- **A.** Whichever has the most downstream consequences if delayed → Truth-Seeker/Builder
- **B.** Whichever affects the most people or has the clearest human cost → Supporter
- **C.** Whichever I can actually complete today — finished beats started → Starter
- **D.** Whichever best advances the long-term strategy → Builder
- **E.** Whichever opens up the most new possibilities when done → Builder/Explainer

### Q7 — Ideal week design

> You're designing how your ideal week would look. What does success feel like at the end of it?

- **A.** I understood something deeply that I didn't before → Truth-Seeker/Explainer
- **B.** I moved key relationships forward in a meaningful way → Supporter
- **C.** I shipped things — real, tangible progress was made → Starter
- **D.** I made decisions that positioned us better for the future → Builder
- **E.** I explored something new that opened up a direction I hadn't seen → Builder/Explainer

## Section D — How You Communicate


### Q8 — Explaining complexity

> You need to explain a complicated situation to someone who doesn't know the background. How do you approach it?

- **A.** Build the logic from the ground up — context first, conclusion last → Truth-Seeker/Explainer
- **B.** Start with the human story — make them feel it before they analyze it → Supporter
- **C.** Start with the three things they need to know and do → Starter
- **D.** Start with where we're trying to go and work backwards → Builder
- **E.** Use an analogy — find the thing they already understand that maps to this → Explainer

## Section E — How You Respond to Conflict


### Q9 — Overruled decision

> A decision you strongly disagree with has just been made above you. What do you do?

- **A.** Request a conversation to understand the reasoning — I may be missing something → Truth-Seeker
- **B.** Assess the impact on the people it affects most and advocate for them → Supporter
- **C.** Comply and execute well — my job is delivery, not governance → Starter/Doer
- **D.** Voice my disagreement clearly once, then commit to the outcome → Truth-Seeker/Builder
- **E.** Look for ways to adapt the decision so it achieves the goal better → Builder/Explainer

### Q10 — Team conflict

> Two people on your team have a serious falling out that's affecting the work. What's your instinct?

- **A.** Understand the factual sequence of events before doing anything → Truth-Seeker
- **B.** Meet with each person separately to hear them fully before any joint conversation → Supporter
- **C.** Address it quickly — the longer it festers, the more damage it does → Starter
- **D.** Clarify expectations and agreements about how the team works together → Builder/Explainer
- **E.** Reframe the shared goal so the conflict becomes less relevant → Builder

## Section F — What You're Curious About


### Q11 — Free Saturday

> You have a completely free Saturday with nothing scheduled. What pulls you most?

- **A.** Going deep on something I've been meaning to understand — reading, researching, learning → Truth-Seeker/Explainer
- **B.** Time with people who matter — meaningful conversation over everything → Supporter
- **C.** Doing something tangible — building, creating, completing a project → Starter/Builder
- **D.** Thinking about the future — journaling, planning, sitting with big questions → Builder
- **E.** Exploring something unfamiliar — a place, idea, or experience I've never had → Builder/Explainer

### Q12 — Fundamental curiosity

> Across all the areas of your life — work, relationships, personal growth — what are you most fundamentally trying to understand?

- **A.** How things actually work — the systems, patterns, and mechanisms underneath → Truth-Seeker
- **B.** How people work — what drives them, what they need, how they change → Supporter
- **C.** How to make things — how to turn ideas into real outcomes in the world → Starter/Builder
- **D.** How to navigate well — how to make wise decisions in an uncertain world → Builder
- **E.** What's possible — what doesn't exist yet that could and should → Builder/Explainer
