# Value Spectrum

*15 questions · about 5 minutes · Single choice · Your Value Score across five dimensions: Self-Worth Baseline, Boundary Intelligence, Comparison Immunity, Value Expression, Identity Ownership*

## Self-Worth Baseline

### Q1 — Baseline Perception

> Left alone with your honest thoughts, no achievements to reference, no roles to hide behind, how do you feel about your inherent worth as a person?

- **A.** I feel fundamentally inadequate. My worth depends on what I produce or achieve. → 0
- **B.** I know I have value but I struggle to feel it consistently without external confirmation. → 3
- **C.** I have a reasonably stable sense of worth that doesn't collapse when things go wrong. → 7
- **D.** My worth is settled and internal. Circumstances don't define it, I do. → 10

### Q2 — Trivialization Response

> You are in a professional or personal relationship where your contribution is consistently minimized, your ideas dismissed, your time treated as disposable. What do you actually do?

- **A.** I accept it. I assume I must be overestimating what I bring. → 0
- **B.** I feel it but don't act, I'm not sure I have the right to object. → 3
- **C.** I name it eventually, though it takes longer than it should. → 7
- **D.** I address it clearly and promptly. My contribution either gets respected or I redirect my investment. → 10

### Q3 — Origin Awareness

> You have a pattern of thinking that limits you, comparison, self-doubt, shrinking in certain contexts. How clearly do you understand where that pattern came from?

- **A.** I know the pattern exists but have not examined where it originated. → 0
- **B.** I have some awareness but the origin feels murky or unresolved. → 3
- **C.** I understand the origin reasonably well and have done meaningful work on it. → 7
- **D.** I understand it clearly. The pattern no longer runs me, I know exactly what it is and where it came from. → 10

## Boundary Intelligence

### Q4 — Boundary Setting

> Someone in your professional life regularly crosses a line, takes credit for your work, demands your time beyond what was agreed, or diminishes you. You:

- **A.** Absorb it. Setting a boundary feels risky and not worth the conflict. → 0
- **B.** Feel the friction but manage it indirectly rather than naming it. → 3
- **C.** Name it, sometimes awkwardly, but I name it and hold it. → 7
- **D.** Set the boundary clearly, once, without apology. The relationship adjusts or I adjust the relationship. → 10

### Q5 — Worth Protection

> A deal, opportunity, or relationship requires you to compromise something fundamental, your time, integrity, standards, or self-respect, in exchange for something you want. You:

- **A.** Take it. What's on offer feels worth the cost and I'm not sure I'll get better. → 0
- **B.** Hesitate but usually take it, telling myself it is temporary. → 3
- **C.** Decline most of the time, though it is genuinely difficult when the offer is significant. → 7
- **D.** Decline without significant internal conflict. What I would give up is not available at any price. → 10

### Q6 — Exit Speed

> After you have clearly identified that your value is being consistently disrespected in a relationship or situation, how long does it take you to act?

- **A.** I recognize it but stay much longer than I should, sometimes indefinitely. → 0
- **B.** I stay for a significant period hoping things will change before I act. → 3
- **C.** I act within a reasonable timeframe once the pattern is clear. → 7
- **D.** I act quickly once the pattern is confirmed. Recognition and response are not far apart. → 10

## Comparison Immunity

### Q7 — Comparison Immunity

> Someone in your field achieves something significant, publicly, visibly, at a level that represents what you want. Your honest internal response in the first 24 hours:

- **A.** I feel diminished. Their achievement highlights my gap and makes me question my path. → 0
- **B.** I feel the pull of comparison but recover and refocus, it just takes a while. → 3
- **C.** I notice it, use it as a reference point, and look for what it confirms. → 7
- **D.** I feel genuinely motivated. External evidence that the destination is real is useful, not threatening. → 10

### Q8 — Cultural Standard Resistance

> The cultural definition of success in your industry looks different from your path. People in your circle are hitting milestones you have not hit. Your experience of that:

- **A.** It affects me significantly. I regularly question whether I am on the right path. → 0
- **B.** It creates noise I have to actively manage to stay focused. → 3
- **C.** I notice it but my internal standard is strong enough not to destabilize me. → 7
- **D.** I don't experience meaningful conflict. My path was set from my own assessment, not theirs. → 10

### Q9 — Isolation Practice

> When evaluating your own value, how deliberately do you separate your self-assessment from external markers of success and other people?

- **A.** I rarely do. My sense of value is largely relative, defined by comparison. → 0
- **B.** I try but the comparison frame reasserts itself quickly. → 3
- **C.** I have a practice for it, intentional reflection or solitude, and it works most of the time. → 7
- **D.** My baseline self-assessment is independent. External markers inform but don't determine my sense of value. → 10

## Value Expression

### Q10 — Transmission Accuracy

> In conversations and situations that matter professionally, how accurately does the way you present yourself reflect how you actually see your own value?

- **A.** There is a significant gap. I project considerably less than I actually believe I bring. → 0
- **B.** I undersell fairly regularly, I know it and am working on it. → 3
- **C.** I present myself reasonably accurately most of the time. → 7
- **D.** What I transmit closely matches what I know about my value. There is no meaningful gap. → 10

### Q11 — Ask Capacity

> You are in a negotiation, salary, rate, partnership terms. The outcome you want is at the upper end of what feels justified. You:

- **A.** Ask for less than I want. Asking for the real number feels presumptuous. → 0
- **B.** Ask for what I want but qualify it heavily, offering discounts before they are requested. → 3
- **C.** Ask for what I want and defend it with evidence when pushed. → 7
- **D.** State the number clearly without qualification. The work speaks for itself. → 10

### Q12 — Authentic Communication

> You disagree with a perspective or decision being made by someone more senior or established than you. What do you actually do?

- **A.** I stay quiet. Disagreeing feels presumptuous or risky. → 0
- **B.** I hint at my position without stating it clearly. Indirect feels safer. → 3
- **C.** I state my position, sometimes with more qualification than the situation requires. → 7
- **D.** I state my position clearly and directly. Rank doesn't determine whose perspective is correct. → 10

## Identity Ownership

### Q13 — Self-Definition

> How much of your current identity, how you see yourself, describe yourself, and carry yourself, was consciously chosen versus inherited from your environment or circumstances?

- **A.** Most of it feels inherited. I haven't examined or made deliberate choices about it. → 0
- **B.** I am aware of the inherited patterns but have not fully moved past them. → 3
- **C.** I have done meaningful work on this. My identity is increasingly chosen rather than inherited. → 7
- **D.** My identity is largely self-defined. I know what I kept, what I released, and why. → 10

### Q14 — Stability Under Pressure

> Under sustained pressure, difficult relationships, public setback, prolonged uncertainty, how stable does your sense of who you are remain?

- **A.** It erodes significantly. Extended difficulty makes me question my fundamental value. → 0
- **B.** It shakes more than I would like. Recovery takes time. → 3
- **C.** It remains mostly stable. I have anchors I return to that hold. → 7
- **D.** It is stable. Who I am doesn't fluctuate with circumstances. → 10

### Q15 — Congruence

> How closely does the person you present publicly, to colleagues, clients, audiences, match the person you actually are in private?

- **A.** There is a significant gap. The public version is considerably more polished or different. → 0
- **B.** There is some gap, certain things I only show privately. → 3
- **C.** I am reasonably congruent. The differences are minor and context-appropriate. → 7
- **D.** There is very little gap. What you see publicly and privately is essentially the same person. → 10
